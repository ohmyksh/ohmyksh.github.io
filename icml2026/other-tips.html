Other Tips

Food and Cafes

Prices

  • Most meals run around 10–20 USD; anything over 30 USD is on the pricier side.
  • Coffee: an Americano costs about 1.5 USD at chains like The Venti, Mega Coffee, Paik's Coffee, and Compose Coffee.
  • Starbucks drinks run 3 to 6 USD.

Ordering System

  • Table service: sit down and press the call button to order. Cash is accepted.
  • Water and side dishes or salad bars are often self-serve, and side dishes are usually free to refill.
  • Kiosks: pay with a physical card, Kakao Pay, or Payco.
  • There is no tipping culture in Korea.

Cafe Culture

  • It's common for people to spend hours working or studying in cafes. Many cafes have power outlets available.
  • If you need a quiet place to work, study cafes (스터디카페) are widely available. They are library-like spaces where you can rent a desk by the hour.

Finding Good Restaurants

  • Naver Map generally has more reviews and is widely used by locals.
  • Instagram tags can be quite useful. Try searching tags like #강남맛집 (Gangnam food) or #성수맛집 (Seongsu food).

Delivery

  • Apps like Baemin and Coupang Eats offer fast delivery, often within 30–60 minutes, depending on the location and time of day.

My personal recommendation: If you want to eat like a Korean college student, grab some Korean fried chicken (there are countless chains, including Kkanbu Chicken), tteokbokki (Dookki or Yupdduk), and dessert at Sulbing or Baskin-Robbins. Then stop by Olive Young or Daiso. :)

Getting Around

Subway

COEX sits at Samseong Station 2 (Exits 5, 6) and Bongeunsa Station 9 (Exit 7), so you will mostly use those two lines. A small fee may apply on some transfers.

  • Seoul Subway App (iOS, English)
  • Official Subway Map (website, English)
  • Naver Subway Map (website, Korean)

Payment

  • Apple Pay is supported at many stores, though not everywhere.
  • A T-money card is required for the subway and buses. Pick one up at any convenience store, or add a virtual card to Apple Wallet if your device supports it.

Map Apps

Naver Map, Google Maps, and Kakao T (taxi).

Transit Notes

  • It's worth checking last train times before heading out.
  • Transfers to a different line or bus are discounted if you tag your card again within 30 minutes of getting off.
  • Rush hour (especially weekday mornings and 5–7 PM) can be quite crowded.
  • Coin lockers are available at major subway stations, useful for storing bags before check-in or after checkout.
  • Bus number colors indicate route type.
  • Priority seats for pregnant passengers and the elderly are kept empty.

Shopping & Convenience

Daiso

Daiso is a great place for inexpensive everyday essentials. You can find almost anything there.

Olive Young

Cosmetics and daily essentials.

Korean Convenience Stores

  • Tasty trendy Korean snacks and drinks.
  • Quick meals to grab on the go.
  • A wide range of everyday items.

Internet

  • Many restaurants and cafes offer free Wi-Fi, often with the network name and password posted inside. If nothing is posted, asking the staff usually works.
  • The Seoul subway offers free public Wi-Fi.
