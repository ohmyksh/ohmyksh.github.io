The Conference

Everything you need to know about getting to COEX and finding your way around.

Getting to COEX

COEX is in the Gangnam business district, directly above Samseong Station (Line 2, green). From Incheon Airport (ICN), the easiest options are:

  • AREX + Line 2: ~80 min, ₩5,000. Take AREX to Seoul Station, transfer to Line 2.
  • Airport Limousine 6103: ~75 min, ₩18,000. Stops near COEX.
  • Taxi / Kakao T: 60–90 min, ₩70–90,000 depending on traffic.

Inside COEX

The convention center sits on top of Starfield COEX Mall, a vast underground complex with restaurants, cafés, an aquarium, and the famous Byeolmadang Library. Useful landmarks:

  • Hall A–E: main sessions and posters
  • Grand Ballroom: keynotes and socials
  • Mall B1/B2: quick lunch options
  • Bongeunsa Temple: across the street, free to visit

By car or taxi

The easiest way to take a taxi is the Kakao T app. It works like Uber, lets you pay in-app, and avoids language barriers. Standard taxis can also be hailed on the street.

Drop-off points around COEX include the East Gate, the Trade Tower, and the Grand InterContinental driveway. Common routes from:

  • Gangnam Station · Daechi-dong · Jamsil Station
  • Gangnam Express Bus Terminal
  • Across the Yeongdong Bridge from northern Seoul

Paid parking is available under COEX (~₩1,000 per 10 min). Traffic peaks 8–10 a.m. and 6–8 p.m.

By bicycle

A public bicycle rack is available at the Trade Tower on the COEX grounds. Seoul's Ttareungi public bike share is available throughout Gangnam. Download the Seoul Bike app and rent for as little as ₩1,000 per hour.

The Han River bike paths are ~15 min south by bike, and connect to most of central Seoul.

Things to Do in Seoul

A handful of neighborhoods and sights worth your free afternoon or post-conference day.

Gyeongbokgung & Bukchon

The main Joseon-era palace. Free entry if you arrive wearing rented hanbok. Catch the changing-of-the-guard ceremony at 10:00 or 14:00. Walk uphill afterward into Bukchon Hanok Village for quiet alleys of traditional houses.

Gyeongbokgung Station, Line 3

Seongsu-dong

Often called "the Brooklyn of Seoul." Former shoe factories turned into cafés, galleries, and concept stores. Good for an afternoon of wandering, with Anthracite, Nudake, and Daelim Changgo nearby.

Seongsu Station, Line 2

Han River at Night

Take Line 5 or 9 to a riverside park (Yeouido, Ttukseom, or Banpo). Grab convenience-store ramen and a beer, watch the Banpo Bridge Rainbow Fountain show (20:00, 21:00 nightly).

Yeouinaru / Ttukseom Resort Station

N Seoul Tower & Namsan

City views from the top of Namsan Mountain. Walk up from Myeongdong (~30 min) or take the cable car. Best at sunset, just before the city lights up.

Myeongdong Station, Line 4

Insadong & Ikseon-dong

Traditional tea houses, calligraphy shops, and souvenirs in Insadong; tiny renovated hanok bars and dessert spots in neighboring Ikseon-dong. Best in the early evening.

Anguk Station, Line 3

Hongdae & Yeonnam

Youthful, music-driven district around Hongik University. Live indie shows, late-night bars, and the leafy Gyeongui Line Forest Park stretching into Yeonnam.

Hongik Univ. Station, Line 2 / AREX

Lotte World Tower (Seoul Sky)

123-floor tower with a glass-floor observation deck. Two subway stops from COEX, so it's an easy add-on after sessions end.

Jamsil Station, Line 2 / Line 8

DMZ Day Trip

Half-day or full-day tours from Seoul to the Demilitarized Zone with Korean and English guides. Book at least a few days ahead; bring your passport.

Tours depart central Seoul · 4–8 hrs

Practical Tips

Small things that make a Seoul trip much smoother.

Transit & T-money

Buy a T-money card at any convenience store (₩4,000) and top it up with cash. It works on the subway, all buses, and most taxis. The subway is the fastest way to get almost anywhere in central Seoul.

Tip: There is also a "Climate Card": unlimited transit for ₩65,000 / 30 days.

Money & Cards

Most places accept Visa/Mastercard, but small restaurants and markets are sometimes cash-only. Use Citibank or KEB Hana ATMs for foreign cards. Tipping is not expected anywhere.

Connectivity

Easiest option: an eSIM from Airalo, Holafly, or KT roaming (~$10–30 for a week). Pocket WiFi is also available at the airport. Public WiFi is widely available but inconsistent.

Weather in early July

Hot and humid: typically 24–30 °C with high humidity. Early July falls inside the jangma monsoon, so expect short, heavy rain showers most days. Bring a compact umbrella and clothes that breathe; indoor air conditioning is strong.

Shopping

  • Hyundai Department Store COEX: connected to the venue.
  • Starfield COEX Mall: basics, electronics, gifts.
  • Myeongdong: cosmetics, street food.
  • Gwangjang & Dongdaemun: markets, late-night clothing.
  • Tax refund: look for the Tax Free sticker; refund at the airport or via kiosk.

Etiquette

  • Take off your shoes when entering a home or traditional restaurant.
  • Receive and give things (cards, money) with two hands.
  • It's fine to be loud at a BBQ table; not on the subway.
  • "감사합니다" (kam-sa-ham-ni-da) = thank you. Goes a long way.

Emergencies

  • 112: police
  • 119: fire / ambulance
  • 1330: 24/7 tourist hotline in English
  • Severance, Asan, and Samsung Medical Center all have international clinics.

A few Korean phrases

  • 안녕하세요 annyeong-haseyo: hello
  • 감사합니다 kamsa-hamnida: thank you
  • 여기요 yeogi-yo: excuse me (to call a waiter)
  • 계산해주세요 gyesan-hae-juseyo: the bill, please
  • 맛있어요 mas-isseoyo: it's delicious
